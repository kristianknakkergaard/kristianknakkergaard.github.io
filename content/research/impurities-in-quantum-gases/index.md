# Quantum gases

Quantum gases has for the last three decades evolved to be an extraordinarily versatile platform to controllably realise intriguing phases of matter, such as Bose-Einstein condensates, degenerate Fermi gases, quantum droplets, supersolids and more. 

# Impurity physics

One area in which these systems have seen their perhaps most precise formulation is in the study of paradigmatic quantum impurity models. A central organising principle has here been the quasi-particle picture of polarons, describing impurities dressed by excitations of the quantum medium. In this manner, both impurities in Fermi and Bose gases have been studied in detail, leading, respectively, to the formation of Fermi and Bose polarons. More recently, the first evidence for induced interactions between Fermi polarons, via the exchange of exciations in the medium, have also been identified. 


## My Contributions

I developed the first dynamical description of the formation of Bose polarons in the weak coupling regime. Based on this, my experimental collaborators designed a setup to investigate this dynamics from the weak to strong coupling regime. Inspired by these experiments, we derived exact formulae for the impurity dynamics at short times. We were, in this manner, able to discriminate between different dynamical regimes leading to the formation and eventual vanishing of the Bose polaron quasiparticles. 

Recently, we pushed this phenomenology to address a long-standing open question about whether there could be low-lying impurity states below these weakly dressed Bose polarons. While the evidence so far is not strong enough to rule out other scenarios, they unambigously do observe states with energies far below that of weakly dressed single Bose polarons. 

## Key Publications

- Skou, M. G. et al. "Non-equilibrium quantum dynamics and formation of the Bose polaron* (2021) - Experimental observation of dynamical regimes leading to the formation of Bose polarons
- Morgen, A. M. et al. "Observation of low-lying impurity states in Bose-Einstein condensates" (2026) - We identify impurity states below the weakly dressed Bose polaron. We argue that the results are best explained by bound states of two polarons, i.e. bipolarons.
- **Nielsen, K.K.** et al. "Critical slowdown of non-equilibrium polaron dynamics" *New Journal of Physics* (2019) - Studies the Bose polaron formation dynamics in the weak coupling regime.
- **Nielsen, K.K.** et al. "Superfluid Flow of Polaron Polaritons above Landau’s Critical Velocity" (2020) - Demonstrates that the superfluid response of the Bose-Einstein condensate can be manipulated to allow polaron-polaritons to travel much faster than the speed of sound.
- Morgen, A. M. et al. "Quantum beat spectroscopy of repulsive Bose polarons" (2025) - Interferometric spectroscopy of repulsive polarons via beat spectroscopy.
