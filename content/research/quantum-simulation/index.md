# Quantum Simulation

Quantum simulation involves using controllable quantum systems to model and understand complex quantum phenomena that are difficult or impossible to study with classical computers. My research focuses on using ultracold atoms and optical lattices to simulate strongly correlated quantum many-body systems.

## My Contributions

I develop theoretical frameworks and numerical methods for quantum simulation experiments, with particular emphasis on:

- **Dual spectroscopy techniques** for probing quasiparticle excitations in quantum gases
- **Polaron-polariton physics** in subwavelength atomic arrays
- **Magnetic polaron formation** in doped Fermi-Hubbard systems

## Key Publications

- **Nielsen, K.K.** et al. "Dual Spectroscopy of Quantum Simulated Fermi-Hubbard Systems" *Physical Review Letters* (2025) - Demonstrates how radio-frequency spectroscopy can reveal magnetic polaron quasiparticles in doped quantum systems
- **Nielsen, K.K.** et al. "Polaron-Polaritons in Subwavelength Arrays of Trapped Atoms" (2026) - Shows how atomic motion couples to collective excitations in optical lattices
- Yang, M. et al. "Beyond fragmented dopant dynamics in quantum spin lattices" *Physical Review B* (2025) - Large-scale matrix-product-state calculations of dopant dynamics
