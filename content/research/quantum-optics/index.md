Quantum optics is concerned with the study of light-matter interactions in the quantum regime. One of the main drivers of this field is to establish controlled interfaces between light and matter, by which either desirable properties of the light or the matter may be achieved. This includes the possibility of long-lived **quantum memories of light**, the achievement of induced **photon-photon interactions**, the possibility of light-altered material properties and much more.  

My research focuses on the intersection of quantum optics with condensed matter physics and quantum simulation. 

## My Contributions

I have contributed to quantum optics research through:

- **Polaron-polariton theory** for subwavelength arrays
- **Analysis of motional effects** on the otherwise pristine mirror properties of subwavelength arrays 
- **Development of spectroscopic techniques** for quantum gas microscopy
- **Investigation of collective excitations** in trapped atom systems
- **Realisation of polaron-polaritons** in Bose-Einstein condensates, and the associated possibility of a light-induced superfluid response
